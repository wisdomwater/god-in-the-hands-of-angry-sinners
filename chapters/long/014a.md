# Part VI

## The Image Beneath the Images

We have spent this book walking through many images of God.

Some have felt ancient and severe. Others have been gentle and inviting. Some have spoken the language of law, others of belonging, reason, beauty, freedom, compassion, or restoration. Each has revealed something true. Each has also left something unseen. Like light passing through a prism, the one white light has appeared in many colors. None of those colors is the whole light, yet neither are they illusions. They are genuine expressions of something larger than themselves.

If we have learned anything along the way, it is that we become like the image of God we behold. The God we fear shapes the way we treat our enemies. The God we trust shapes the way we endure suffering. The God we worship shapes the way we love our neighbors. Whether we know it or not, every image carries within it the seeds of a particular kind of life.

Yet as we have moved from chapter to chapter, another pattern has slowly emerged. Beneath every image lies a deeper longing. The Wrathful God longs to protect what is precious. The Tribal God longs to gather people into belonging. The King God longs to establish justice and peace. The God of Control longs for a universe that is neither chaotic nor abandoned. The God of Success longs for life to flourish. The God of Reason longs for truth. The God of Certainty longs for a firm foundation. The God of Meaning longs for purpose. The God of Beauty longs to awaken wonder. The God of Freedom longs to release what has been bound. The God of Compassion longs to enter another's suffering. The God of Restoration longs to heal what has been broken.

Each of these longings points beyond itself.

What if they are not separate destinations at all? What if they are tributaries flowing toward the same sea?

It is tempting to imagine that some of these images stand in opposition to love. Wrath is often placed against mercy. Justice is contrasted with compassion. Truth is imagined to compete with kindness. Freedom is set against responsibility. Yet these oppositions arise only when one image is torn away from its source. A justice that is not rooted in love ceases to be justice and becomes vengeance. Freedom without love becomes indifference. Compassion without love can collapse into sentimentality. Truth without love becomes a weapon. Even restoration, severed from love, can become little more than fixing what is useful rather than cherishing what is wounded.

Love is not one virtue among many. It is the life from which the virtues draw their breath.

This is why the Christian scriptures can make such astonishing claims. They do not merely say that God is loving, as though love were one quality among many. They dare to say that God is love. If that is true, then every authentic glimpse of God is already a glimpse of love, even if it has not yet learned to speak that name. Every true image is rooted in love, nourished by love, and finds its fulfillment in love.

This does not mean that love is sentimental or naïve. Love sometimes comforts, but it also confronts. It forgives, yet it also tells the truth. It is patient, but it refuses to abandon what is good. Love can be gentle enough to bind a wound and fierce enough to stand between a child and danger. It can weep beside the suffering while also laboring to transform the conditions that caused their suffering. Love is not the opposite of justice. It is the reason justice exists. Love is not the enemy of truth. It is truth's deepest purpose. Love is not what remains after strength has been softened. It is the strength from which all holy action flows.

Seen this way, the previous chapters are transformed. They are no longer competing portraits of God but windows looking toward the same horizon. Each window frames a different part of the landscape. Each allows light to enter. None can contain the sky.

Perhaps this has always been the invitation. Not to choose between wrath and compassion, reason and beauty, certainty and freedom, but to discover the love that gives each of them its proper place. Like instruments in a great orchestra, every image finds its harmony only when it plays the deeper music from which it came.

As we enter this final part of the journey, we are no longer asking which image is correct. We are asking what gives every true image its life. We are looking for the heartbeat beneath every virtue, the current beneath every river, the fire beneath every facet.

We are looking for love.
