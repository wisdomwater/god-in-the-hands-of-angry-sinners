# God in the Hands of Angry Sinners

*A contemplative exploration of the images of God that shape the human soul.*

## Overview

This repository contains the manuscript for **God in the Hands of Angry Sinners**, a contemplative work exploring one simple but profound idea:

> **We become like what we worship.**

Throughout history, people have imagined God in many different ways—as judge, king, protector, provider, liberator, healer, restorer, and love itself. Each of these images reveals something true about the divine while also remaining incomplete. Every image becomes a lens through which we understand both God and ourselves.

Rather than asking which image is "correct," this book asks a different question:

> **What kind of person does this image of God form?**

Each chapter explores one image through six movements:

* **Portrait** — How God appears through this image.
* **Worship** — How this image shapes devotion and daily life.
* **Gift** — The genuine truth and beauty it preserves.
* **Shadow** — What happens when one facet of God is mistaken for the whole.
* **Disciple** — The kind of person this image tends to form.
* **Reflection** — An invitation toward deeper contemplation.

The journey moves from humanity's earliest survival-oriented images of God through increasingly expansive visions of achievement, understanding, experience, relationship, and finally love, before ending with the recognition that God ultimately transcends every image we can conceive.

## A Note to the Reader

Although written from within the Christian contemplative tradition, this book is intended for readers of every background.

The question it asks is ultimately a human one.

Whether our highest devotion is directed toward God, success, certainty, beauty, freedom, compassion, or some other ultimate value, our deepest commitments quietly shape the people we become.

This book is therefore less interested in defending a particular theological system than in exploring the transformative power of the images that guide our lives.

## About the Title

The title is an intentional conversation with Jonathan Edwards' famous sermon *Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God*.

This work is not written as a rebuttal to Edwards, nor as a critique of those who find meaning in his vision. Rather, it asks how different images of God shape the human heart. The goal is understanding rather than argument, curiosity rather than caricature.

*"Every true image of God is a window. The purpose of the window is not to be admired, but to help us see the light beyond it."*
